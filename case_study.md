# Mortgage-Backed Securities (MBS) Case Study

## What I tried to understand

Before starting with the dataset, I wanted to get a basic idea of what Mortgage-Backed Securities (MBS) are.

From what I understood, banks give home loans to individuals. Instead of waiting for years to receive repayments, they bundle these loans together and sell them as securities to investors.

So in a way, investors are indirectly investing in a pool of home loans.

---

## Why MBS is important

I think MBS plays an important role in financial systems because:

- It provides liquidity to banks so they can issue more loans
- Investors get returns based on loan repayments
- It helps in distributing financial risk across multiple participants

---

## Risk factors I noticed

While going through the concept, I came across some key risks:

- **Default Risk** → Borrowers may fail to repay loans  
- **Prepayment Risk** → Borrowers may repay early, reducing expected returns  
- **Interest Rate Risk** → Changes in interest rates affect loan behavior  

Out of these, I feel default risk is most relevant for our analysis.

---

## Connecting this to our dataset

Looking at the dataset, I think the following features are important:

- **Credit Score** → Indicates borrower reliability  
- **DTI (Debt-to-Income ratio)** → Shows financial burden  
- **Loan Amount** → Higher loan amounts may increase risk  
- **Loan Term** → Longer duration could increase uncertainty  

I feel these variables will play a major role in predicting loan risk.

---

## My understanding of the problem

From this case study, I think our main goal is:

👉 To predict whether a loan is risky or not using borrower and loan-related features.

---

## What I found interesting

I found it interesting that simple home loans can be converted into financial instruments and traded in the market.

Also, small factors like credit score or income level can influence large-scale financial decisions.

---

## My thoughts

I think if we can accurately identify risky loans:

- Banks can reduce losses  
- Investors can make better decisions  
- Overall system can become more stable  

---

## Limitations

This is a basic understanding of MBS, and real-world systems are much more complex.

There might be additional macroeconomic factors like inflation, unemployment, and market conditions that are not considered here.
